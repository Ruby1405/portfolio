Bloodhound

A high resolution model for still frame renders. It resembles a glaive used by a D&D character

Most of the model has 1 or 2 levels of subdivision surface. The tongues are made using path curves extruding round profiles. The initial plan for the spine/pole was to have a geometry nodes modifier for placing vertebrae along a curve and and through shape keys control control how developed the back of each vertebrae is. Shape keys are however evaluated before modifiers and can therefore not be controlled by geometry nodes. The vertebrae were therefore manually placed scaled.

It would be possible to make a collection of differently developed vertebrae and still use geometry nodes to place them but that would complicate scaling of the backs of the vertebrae.
